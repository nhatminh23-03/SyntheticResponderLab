## Section C: Product Stimulus

**[Respondents read the following product description before answering.]**

**Product Description — Tahoe Mini by Neo Smart Living**

[Product image]

*[Fallback link if image rendering is blocked: Tahoe Mini model page]*

The Tahoe Mini is a compact 117-square-foot factory-built backyard unit — designed as a private, standalone studio, workspace, or guestroom. It measures approximately 9 ft × 13 ft (roughly the footprint of a standard backyard storage shed) and features a bright, open layout. It is delivered as flat-packed panels and can be installed in most backyards — even those with limited access — typically within one day. The approximate price is $23,000 including sales tax, delivery, installation, and warranty.

A key differentiator of the Tahoe Mini is its modular construction system. The wall panels are interchangeable and configurable, allowing customers to customize window placement, wall composition, and layout orientation based on their specific backyard setup and intended use. This modular flexibility allows the structure to adapt to different lifestyle needs — whether maximizing natural light, increasing privacy, or optimizing wall space for shelving, desks, or creative equipment.

The Tahoe Mini is not an ADU (Accessory Dwelling Unit). It does not include plumbing or a kitchen and is classified as a non-habitable accessory structure — a premium modular space, not a permitted living unit. At 117 sq ft, it is intentionally sized under California's ~120 sq ft threshold, which means it often does not require a building permit (though local city ordinances and HOA rules should always be verified).

Standard features include pre-wired electrical, smart entry lock, dual-pane floor-to-ceiling glass, a pitched roof with drainage, and optional upgrades such as sound insulation and heating/cooling.
